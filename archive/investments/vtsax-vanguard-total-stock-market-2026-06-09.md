# Vanguard Total Stock Market Index Fund (VTSAX)

## Summary
VTSAX is a broad US equity index fund managed by Vanguard that tracks the performance of the entire US stock market, covering small-, mid-, and large-cap stocks. The family holds approximately $2.4 million across two Vanguard accounts. With an annual expense ratio of just 0.04%, this is one of the lowest-cost ways to own a diversified slice of US equities — at the current balance, annual fund fees amount to roughly $960. The position has been held since 2011, when the family's father deliberately moved assets out of an actively managed fund and into this passive index fund.
